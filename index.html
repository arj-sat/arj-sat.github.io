01 — About

Building at the edge of perception.

Robotics Computer Vision Neuromorphic Event Cameras Automation Startup

I'm a Masters student of Computing at the Australian National University, where my research sits at the frontier of robotics perception. My work on event compression for visual place recognition tackles one of the hardest open problems in autonomous navigation — enabling robots to reliably recognise previously visited locations under changing real-world conditions.

Outside academia, I'm trying to build CognIA — a startup channelling my research curiosity into real-world intelligent systems. I believe the most interesting things happen when rigorous science meets entrepreneurial urgency.

I bring three years of hands-on automation experience to everything I build — a grounding in systems thinking that shapes how I approach both engineering and research problems.
